Halloween

Halloween is thought to have influences from Christian beliefs and practices. The English word 'Halloween' comes from "All Hallows' Eve", being the evening before the Christian holy days of All Hallows' Day (All Saints' Day) on 1 November and All Souls' Day on 2 November. Halloween, a holiday observed on October 31 and noted for its pagan and religious roots and secular traditions. In popular culture, the day has become a celebration of horror and is associated with the macabre and the supernatural. Haunted house, Trick or treating, Halloween movie night, Halloween party, Pumpkin carving, Visit a pumpkin patch, Apple bobbing, Corn maze, and Boo your neighbors are traditional activities. Pumpkin Pie, Pumpkin Bread, Caramel Apples, Apple Bread, Caramel Corn, Candy Corn, Soul Cakes, and Devil's Food Cupcakes are Halloween food and customs.
