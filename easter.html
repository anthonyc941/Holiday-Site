Easter

Well, it turns out Easter actually began as a pagan festival celebrating spring in the Northern Hemisphere, long before the advent of Christianity. "Since pre-historic times, people have celebrated the equinoxes and the solstices as sacred times," University of Sydney Professor Carole Cusack said. Easter is on the april 20 and is in commemorating the Resurrection of Jesus, Easter also celebrates the defeat of death and the hope of salvation. Christian tradition holds that the sins of humanity were paid for by the death of Jesus and that his Resurrection represents the anticipation believers can have in their own resurrection. Easter Traditions for families: Plant an Easter Garden, decorate an Easter Egg Tree, Journey to Easter through Scripture, Gather Round the Table, Baking, Observe Lent, and attend an Easter Parade. Easter Food Traditions From Around the World:Eggs which is One of the most recognized Easter symbols, Lamb, Ham, Bun and Cheese, Hot Cross Buns, Enriched Breads, East Indian Fugias, Difo Dabo.
